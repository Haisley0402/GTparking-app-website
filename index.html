P‑1 · The Problem

Parking shouldn't be the hardest part of your day.

Focus Question

How can we reduce the stress and uncertainty Georgia Tech students, faculty, staff, and visitors experience when finding and managing campus parking?

Target Users & Context

Students Faculty Staff Visitors

Context of use: parking on and around Georgia Tech's campus — from daily commuting to one-off event visits.

Focus Group

6participants
All current students 3 of 6 hold permits

P‑2 · Listening First

We wrote a discussion guide before we sketched a single screen.

A 45-minute focus group moved from easy, factual warm-ups into open-ended pain-point discussion, then handed participants a few hands-on probes.

01 · Intro

Demographics

  • Age
  • Student / staff / faculty / visitor
  • Any accessibility needs
02 · Warm-up

Factual

  • How long have you parked on/around campus?
  • How often do you come to campus?
  • Ever purchased a GT parking pass?
  • Where do you normally park?
03 · Main

Open-ended

  • Your single largest parking pain point?
  • Has parking ever made you late or stuck in traffic?
  • Biggest improvement, besides “more spots”?
04 · Probes

Design Probes

  • Draw where you park most often
  • Document a typical parking day
  • Observe difficulties during busy events

P‑4 · User Needs

Five needs rose to the top.

01

Needing to know how full a parking lot is

Whether a lot or deck is worth the drive depends entirely on how full it already is — right now, drivers find out by showing up.

Affinity theme: issues navigating parking spots/lots
02

Needing to know how construction or traffic impacts the lot

Blocked access points and roadwork around campus make it hard to even reach a lot, let alone find a spot in it.

Affinity theme: issues navigating parking spots/lots
03

Needing to know if an event is going to take your parking

Permit holders described being bumped from their usual deck to make room for guests arriving for games and campus events.

Affinity theme: non-routine issues with parking
04

Needing to know where to park as a visitor

First-time visitors have no reliable way to find the right lot, let alone know it's open to them at all.

Affinity theme: issues with actual GT permit
05

Needing the best place to park for the day's schedule — and what it costs to move

Back-to-back classes scattered across campus mean one spot rarely works all day; drivers need to know if moving is worth the price.

Affinity theme: georgia tech (PTS) sucks

P‑5 · What We Designed Toward

Six features, each tracing back to a need.

Ranked by focus-group votes, every feature on this list exists to answer one of the five needs above — nothing was added just because it sounded nice.

Capacity Monitor

Live fullness for every lot and deck, visible before you ever leave home.

Answers Need 01

Construction Tracker

Flags blocked access points and roadwork on the way to your usual spot.

Answers Need 02

Event Parking Alerts

Same-day notice when an event will claim your deck — plus where to go instead.

Answers Need 03

Visitor Parking Guidance

A guest-friendly view that needs no permit history to find the nearest open lot.

Answers Need 04

Live Pricing per Space

Price shown next to every lot, so “cheapest open spot” is one glance away.

Answers Need 04 & 05

Schedule-Based Optimizer

Pins the best spot against today's class schedule — and what it costs to relocate later.

Answers Need 05

P‑6 · Personas & Scenarios

Two commuters, two very different mornings.

GT BUZZCARD3RD YR

Jane Doe

Age 20 · In-state · Undergraduate, 3rd year
15-min drive from campus GT permit, 2 years Parks in Family Deck

Pain Points

  • No information about construction that blocks her usual route
  • Has to scan her buzzcard manually instead of automatic license-plate reading
  • Gets kicked out of parking during campus sporting events
SCENARIO 01 EVENT PARKING

There's a basketball game at McCamish tonight at 7 PM. At 9 AM, Jane gets a notification from GT Park that she'll need to move her car to make room for visitor parking. She checks the app for additional availability to replace her usual spot. After 5 PM, she sees open parking in Peters Parking Deck and moves her car there successfully.

VISITOR PASS2ND YR GRAD

Michael Turner

Age 24 · Out of state · Graduate student, 2nd year
Lives in West Midtown Parks in apartment lot Drives to campus sometimes

Pain Points

  • No permit, so no access to permit lots
  • Sometimes struggles to find parking on campus for events he's attending
  • Doesn't know the optimal place to park based on distance and price
SCENARIO 02 EVENT AT MCCAMISH

Michael decides to attend a GT basketball game tonight. Arriving a little late, he knows the lots closest to McCamish will already be full. He opens GT Park, enters McCamish as his destination, and the app surfaces nearby guest lots with capacity and pricing. He compares the options and parks in the cheapest open lot.
